# Luxora Style Direction

## Brand Mood

Luxora should feel cinematic, precise, and quietly expensive. The mood is premium mobile technology presented with the restraint of a high-end boutique. The first viewport can be atmospheric and dramatic, but the rest of the page should support confident browsing and buying.

Core mood words:

- Cinematic.
- Restrained.
- Polished.
- Modern.
- Tactile.
- Trustworthy.
- Practical.

## Color Direction

Use a dark atmospheric hero with controlled highlights. After the hero, transition into clean shopping surfaces that feel bright enough to browse but still premium.

Preferred direction:

- Deep graphite, near-black, and soft charcoal for the hero.
- Clean off-white, cool gray, and muted neutral surfaces for shopping sections.
- Small metallic accents such as champagne, platinum, or cool silver.
- Occasional saturated color only when tied to product imagery or clear actions.

Avoid building the entire site around one heavy hue. The page should not become all purple, all beige, all blue, or all black.

## Typography Feel

Typography should feel modern, confident, and highly legible.

- Use large type only where it earns the space, especially in the hero.
- Use compact, scannable type for product cards, navigation, filters, prices, and trust content.
- Keep letter spacing natural.
- Prefer short headlines with precise supporting copy.
- Product and commerce text should be clearer than the atmospheric copy.

## Spacing Philosophy

Spacing should create calm and hierarchy without wasting the mobile viewport.

- Give the hero enough room to feel cinematic.
- Keep shopping sections tighter and easier to scan.
- Use consistent section rhythm after the hero.
- Keep product grids and category access efficient on mobile.
- Avoid oversized cards inside other cards.
- Let imagery breathe, but keep actions close to the buying context.

## Animation Philosophy

Animation should support desire, clarity, and confidence.

- Use GSAP later only for the hero or serious scroll storytelling.
- Use Motion later for small UI transitions such as menus, product reveals, or microinteractions.
- Use Swiper later for product carousels when carousel behavior is actually needed.
- Do not add these dependencies until specifically requested.
- Keep core shopping usable without animation.
- Prefer subtle fades, gentle transforms, and responsive feedback over constant movement.

The homepage should feel cinematic at the top, then behave like a real e-commerce site.

## Examples Of What To Avoid

- A full-page motion demo where shopping is secondary.
- Generic startup landing-page composition.
- Overly glossy discount-store visuals.
- Too many gradients, glows, or decorative background shapes.
- Product cards that hide price, specs, or purchase actions.
- Carousels that trap important content.
- Dense promotional clutter above product discovery.
- Heavy animation on every scroll section.
- Long blocks of brand copy before categories or products.

## E-Commerce Usability Principles

- Make the first shopping path obvious immediately after the hero.
- Show categories early.
- Show featured products with clear names, prices, and actions.
- Keep mobile tap targets comfortable.
- Use product imagery as the primary selling signal.
- Make promotions feel premium, not shouty.
- Keep trust signals short and specific.
- Keep navigation predictable.
- Preserve fast scanning from section to section.
- Favor clarity over novelty when a user is deciding what to buy.
